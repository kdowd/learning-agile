Principle #8:

Sustainable development - maintaining progress

The eighth Agile principle emphasizes promoting sustainable development, where sponsors, developers, and users can maintain a constant, indefinite pace. This principle counters the common issue of teams working unsustainable "insane hours" due to unrealistic deadlines, a tactic often employed in traditional "command-and-control" management. While short bursts of overtime can boost productivity, extended periods lead to decreased output, lower quality, and team burnout. Agile teams prioritize a sustainable pace by planning work that can realistically be completed within defined iterations (e.g., two to six weeks). This iterative approach makes estimation more accurate and helps avoid the necessity of frequent late nights and weekends, fostering a healthier and ultimately more productive work environment.
