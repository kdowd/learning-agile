Principle #4:

Face-to-face is communication is best

The fourth Agile principle asserts that face-to-face conversation is the most efficient and effective method for conveying information within a development team.

While documentation serves as a communication tool, its primary goal is shared understanding, which often benefits more from direct interaction. Agile practices prioritize verbal communication, reserving detailed documentation for complex information needing later recall.

Teams accustomed to comprehensive documentation often engage in face-to-face discussions regardless, recognizing its superior effectiveness in bridging understanding gaps.

By fostering a sense of community and shared perspective, teams develop implied knowledge, reducing the need for repetitive explanations and enabling more efficient handling of changes and problem-solving, moving them past documentation management to focus on code.
