Principle #11:

Self-organizing teams.

The eleventh Agile principle states that the best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams. This challenges the traditional waterfall approach, where dedicated phases for requirements and design often lead to overly complex, upfront planning and extensive documentation. Self-organizing teams, by contrast, collaboratively plan and continually revise their approach, breaking projects into small, valuable chunks and addressing detailed requirements and design iteratively. This shift means that while a senior architect still plays a crucial role, their work becomes more integrated and less isolated. Instead of creating a single, comprehensive upfront design, agile architects engage in incremental design, ensuring the system is not only complete but also adaptable to ongoing changes, ultimately fostering more robust and flexible outcomes.
